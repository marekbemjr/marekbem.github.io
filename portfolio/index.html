Introduce

My name is Marek Bem. I am an experienced Global Share Plan Manager and Vesting Specialist with a strong background in data analysis and client relationship management. With over five years' experience in international companies, I effectively organise and analyse data sets using tools such as SQL and Excel. Known for my critical thinking, communication skills and adaptability, I provide tailored solutions to client needs and build lasting business relationships. I am currently furthering my qualifications on the Business Analyst course at Future Collars, where I am developing an app focused on wine tourism in Poland, combining my passion for innovation with industry knowledge. In my spare time I enjoy enotourism, especially discovering Polish wines, capturing urban life through street photography and exploring the culture and landscapes of Turkey.

Experience

Global Share Plan Manager and Data Analyst with over five years' experience working for international companies. I am skilled in managing remuneration share plans and have experience in analysing datasets using SQL, Excel and Python. I am adept at communicating insights, building relationships with clients and applying structured, methodical approaches to work. I recently started a Business Analyst Bootcamp with Future Collars to further develop my understanding of business analysis principles, stakeholder management and problem solving within projects. Most recently, I worked at Equatex Global, where I managed complex share plan projects, ensuring that shares were distributed to participants in accordance with client requirements, while working with internal and external stakeholders to optimise system configurations and processes. In my previous role as a Client Solution Consultant at NielsenIQ, I managed international client accounts, oversaw the delivery of FMCG databases and designed and developed databases to meet the evolving needs of clients. I am a strong communicator, able to translate client needs into operational terms and have effectively implemented workflow procedures to improve team productivity. My analytical skills were honed during my time as a Data Processing Specialist at NielsenIQ, where I worked to ensure data accuracy and identify process improvements. Outside of work, I have a passion for wine tourism, particularly Polish wines, and enjoy street photography, capturing candid moments and urban scenes. I also have a deep interest in Turkish culture and regularly explore the country's rich history and landscapes. With a Master's degree in Hospitality and Tourism Management from the Warsaw School of Economics, I combine my analytical skills with a passion for customer service and problem solving. I have also completed several data analytics courses, including Google’s Data Analytics Professional Certificate, Duke University’s course on Tableau, and IBM’s Data Analysis with R. I am motivated, well-organized, and adapts easily to changing environments, with a strong ability to use tools like Salesforce, Jira, Confluence, and ServiceNow.

About Me

I really love that the data analysis is all about digging into things, learning more about them and improving them. It's like working with puzzles, and when you finish the puzzle, you get to help companies make smarter business decisions! In my spare time, I love street photography. Street photography is a great way for me to focus on people and their behaviour in public, which is also a wonderful way of recording people's history. I also love spending my free time actively playing basketball or hiking. But my absolute passion is travelling! Recently, I've been really interested in enotourism, with a particular focus on Polish wines. I just love learning about them and promoting their unique qualities!
